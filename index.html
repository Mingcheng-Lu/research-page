I am an undergraduate student at the School of Mathematical Sciences, Fudan University. My research focuses on the theoretical foundations of machine learning. My long-term goal is to develop efficient and interpretable AI systems. Recently, I mainly study the statistical rates of flow matching models.

Research

My work focuses on the theoretical foundations of machine learning, especially for generative models and large-scale systems. Currently, I'm mentored by Han Liu.

Research Interest

My research interests lie in machine learning theory, with a focus on the foundations of large language models and generative AI.

Research Experience and Ongoing Projects

  • A Theoretical Analysis of Discrete Flow Matching Generative Models. We provide a theoretical analysis for end-to-end training Discrete Flow Matching (DFM) generative models. Specifically, we develop the technique of discrete-to-continuous functional extension and bounding discrete distribution estimation error with velocity estimation error.
  • Learning Low-Dimensional Manifold Data with Flow-Matching Transformers. We study the statistical rates and minimax optimal estimation when data lie on a low-dimensional manifold, examining how latent dimension and smoothness of velocity function influence the rates.

Future Research Plan

Given my research experience so far, I plan to further explore machine learning theory in LLMs and GenAI, while seeking opportunities to bridge theory and practice.
