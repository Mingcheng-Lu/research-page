I am an undergraduate student at the School of Mathematical Sciences, Fudan University. My research focuses on the theoretical foundations of machine learning. My long-term goal is to develop efficient and interpretable AI systems. Recently, I mainly study the statistical rates of flow matching models.

Research

My work focuses on the theoretical foundations of machine learning, especially for generative models and large-scale systems. I am currently mentored by Jerry Yao-Chieh Hu , a Ph.D. candidate at Northwestern University.

Research Interest

I am interested in machine learning theory and the theoretical basis of large language models. My current focus is on generative modeling, especially diffusion and flow-matching frameworks.

Research Experience and Ongoing Projects

  • Learning Low-Dimensional Manifold Data with Flow-Matching Transformers(2025.4-ongoing). We study the statistical rates and minimax optimal estimation when data lie on a low-dimensional manifold, examining how latent dimension and smoothness of velocity function influence the rates. I'm responsible for writing several sections of the paper.
  • Statistical rates for Discrete Flow Matching(2025.7-ongoing). We analyze approximation and estimation errors for flows on discrete state spaces (e.g., graphs/categorical data) and aim to reach at a minimax optimal estimation. I am the main author for this project and so far have made most of the contribution.

Future Research Plan

With all the research experience above, I hope to pursue more work in machine-learning theory, especially the foundations of large models. At the same time, I'm also eager to engage in practice-related projects that complement theoretical research.
